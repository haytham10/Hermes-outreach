# The Drafting Doc

*How a finding becomes a line that lands. Source: Harry Dry.*

## The three rules

Run every line through these:
1. **Can I visualize it?** If you can't picture it, she won't remember it.
2. **Can I falsify it?** Provably true or false makes her sit up.
3. **Can nobody else say it?** Bespoke to her specific page.

## Make it concrete (zoom in)

Take the vague word and rewrite until you hit something you can see.
Abstract: "no opt-in capture" → Zoom: people download and leave → Concrete: "49K people go through your flow and you keep none of them"

## Make it falsifiable (point, don't talk)

No adjectives for the leak. Point at the exact thing on the exact page.
Talking: "your checkout has issues" → Pointing: "the flow ends on a Google Drive link, no email, no name, nothing"

## Make it bespoke

If the line could describe 500 coaches, you haven't looked hard enough.

## Conflict — put it in every email

Write it with a hidden "but". The thing was going one way, but — that's the turn.

## Facts — if in doubt, give a fact

Never "your funnel is leaking." Always the specific number, the specific page, the specific thing.

## The quality bar

- 9/10 or it doesn't go.
- One Mississippi test: does it land in two seconds?
- The garden-walk test: done when you can't add or remove a word.
