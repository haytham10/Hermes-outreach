# Email OS — Cold Email Mechanics

The execution layer for cold email outreach. The Bible owns the methodology; this doc owns the channel mechanics.

## Confirmed principles (rules, not suggestions)

- **Offer beats copy.** Outcome + timeframe + risk reversal.
- **Outcome only, never features.** Lead with the result, not the credential.
- **Keep it vague enough to create curiosity.** Name the specific finding, leave the fix vague.
- **Short beats long.** Under 100 words. One finding, one cost, one question.
- **Your finding is a trigger event.** A real leak is a reason to reach out now.
- **Plain text only.** No images, HTML, Loom links, or Calendly in email 1.
- **One CTA only.** One question or one offer per email.
- **Exact numbers over ranges.** "$8,123" beats "multiple five figures."
- **Your opening email IS the lead magnet.** Don't withhold it for a call.
- **Follow-up is where the money is.** 70-80% of positive replies come at touch 3-4 on cold.

## The email motion

1. Source a lead. Collect their email.
2. Walk the funnel (Gate 0 → Gate 1 → 5-stop walk → 2 filters → 3 lanes).
3. Write the email — subject + specific finding + one question. Send.
4. They reply — turn-two: ONE concrete artifact offer or single-tap next step.
5. They say yes to the Loom — record and send same day.
6. Book the call via Calendly link.
7. Call — audit-first, name ONE small project + price, ask yes or no.
8. Yes → deposit — 50/50, deposit before build starts.

## Finding their email (decision tree)

1. Website footer / Contact page
2. Linktree / Beacons / platform page
3. Opt in to their freebie (reply-to = best email)
4. Podcast / YouTube show notes
5. Google search ("[name]" OR "[handle]" email contact parenting coach)
6. Pattern guess + verify (Hunter.io / NeverBounce)
7. Park as Email Not Found

## Writing the email

**Subject line:** Name the specific thing, not the category. Under 8 words. No end punctuation. SMYKM-specific.

**Body:**
- Line 1: the finding, stated flatly. No warm-up.
- Line 2: the felt cost in one sentence.
- Line 3: one question or one concrete offer. Not both.
- Sign off: none (Gmail auto-signature).

**Follow-up sequence (cold):**
- Touch 1: opener
- Touch 2: day 3-4, new angle
- Touch 3: day 6-7, one-liner bump
- Touch 4: day 8-9, final. After 4 with no reply → Dormant (not Lost)

**Warm sequence (replied once):**
- Every 2-3 days. Up to 8-10 touches. Short one-liners, pattern interrupts.
- 80% of warm meetings come between follow-up 5-9.

## Tracking

What to log per send: Date, lead name, email, subject, opened?, replied?, reply type, next action.

Metrics that matter: Positive reply rate > Deposit received > Open rate > Loom acceptance > Call book rate.
