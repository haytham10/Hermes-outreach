# SMYKM Test Layer

*Test variant on top of the Email OS. Source: Sam McKenna "Show Me You Know Me."*

## Why this test exists

Current openers lead with the finding (The Company — their funnel). Sam's method adds **The Human** — researching the person before the funnel, opening on something only they would recognize.

## Rule 1 — SMYKM subject line

The subject must make no sense to anyone except this specific person. References their own framework name, a phrase from their content, a specific episode title.

Good: "the 2 choices framework" / "your episode on eldest kids" / "the NSR method"
Dead: "quick question" / "I noticed something" / "free audit for coaches"

Under 8 words, no end punctuation, no ALL CAPS, sentence case.

## Rule 2 — Niche lingo swap

Never use operator vocabulary:
- "clients"/"leads" → parents / moms / families
- "funnel" / "sequence" / "email capture" / "opt-in" / "conversion" / "optimize" / "audit" — never say it
- "audit" → "I went through your..."

## Rule 3 — First sentence

Subject + first sentence = preview text. Two options:
A — Elaborate on the SMYKM hook, then bridge to finding.
B — Human observation, then transition to finding.

## Rule 4 — Transition line

Only for opening B. Bridges the human hook to the reason you're writing.
- "anyway, the reason I'm writing..."
- "what actually made me reach out though..."

## Rule 5 — Objection pre-handle (Lane 2 especially)

Anticipate the one objection and answer it before they can say it.

## Rule 6 — Close timing

No Calendly link. Broad timeframe: "do you have time over the next week or so?"

## Sequence test — 48-hour second touch

Touch 2 under 48 hours after touch 1, same subject line, same thread.

## The trigger move

When Mailtrack shows multiple opens — that's intent. Use a warm-stage interrupt.

## What stays the same (Email OS)

Finding is real and verified. One question or offer. Under 100 words. Plain text. No Loom in email 1. No Calendly in email 1. No soft exits. Track everything.
