# Cold Touch 1 — Proven Pattern

The structure that passed the register test for Roisin (Beyond Typical Parenting). Use this as a template for cold openers, not as a formula — each element must be bespoke.

## Subject (SMYKM hook)
- References her own CTA, framework name, episode title, or specific phrase from her content
- Under 8 words, sentence case, no end punctuation
- Makes no sense to anyone except her

## Body structure

### Paragraph 1 — Hook elaboration + admire (1-2 sentences)
First sentence MUST echo the SMYKM hook (subject-body continuity).
Second sentence is a short, specific admire. Not a list. Not a three-beat.

```
Your [her CTA / framework] posts work for the [audience] who [action].
[Specific thing about her offer] is good.
```

### Paragraph 2 — Finding + cost (3-5 very short sentences)
State the finding flatly. No discovery frame. No "I noticed." No "I wanted to reach out."
Then the cost in one line. Then a fragment that just stops.

```
The rest of the [N] [verb] though. Nothing to [capture action]. They [leave/disappear].
Next [event], [consequence].
```

**Key rules:**
- "though" does the work of the reluctant transition — no separate transition phrase needed
- "Nothing to [verb] for." is the fragment that stops hard — keep it
- The consequence ("Next launch, clean slate") is the felt cost in human language, not a measurement

### Paragraph 3 — Motivation + open door (1-2 sentences)
One sentence stating the real motivation. Brief.
One sentence making the concrete offer. Conditional ("if you want") + visual picture.

```
I couldn't just watch it. If you want, I can show you what I'd put between [place A] and [place B].
```

**Key rules:**
- "I couldn't just watch it" — "it" is clear from context (the people disappearing). Don't re-explain.
- The concrete visual ("between the feed and a list") is the picture she can see
- "If you want" is warm, not demanding
- "I can show you" is confident, not pushy

## What NOT to do (proven failures)

| Failure | What it sounds like |
|---|---|
| Three-beat admiration | "Your cohort is well built, your voice is consistent, and your niche has weight" |
| Math breakdown | "Of 11,500, 75 liked it, a few dozen will comment, the other 11,000 are not..." |
| Formula transition | "The thing that got me though..." |
| Formula close | "Deliberate, or one of the things that hasn't been built yet?" |
| Discovery frame | "I noticed your site doesn't have..." / "I went through your site and found..." |
| Consultant polish | "The voice carries from the feed to the page, and not many coaches manage that." |
| Soft exits | "Let me know if you'd like to chat" / "If you're open to it, I'd love to show you..." |

## Before submitting

1. Run the automated gate (kill list, em-dash, operator vocab, three-beat check)
2. Run the register test — read it aloud
3. Check: does every sentence stand alone? (burrito test)
4. Check: is there at least one rough edge (fragment, dropped word, line that just stops)?
5. Check: does the subject hook appear in the first body sentence?
